Neil Armstrong

One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind

Neil Armstrong posing in his space suit

  • 1930 - Neil Armstrong is born, August 5th in Ohio
  • 1946 - Neil learns how to fly. At the age of 16 he earned his student pilot's license
  • 1947 - Neil joined the US Navy, and he attended Purdue on scholarship. He studied aeronautical engineering.
  • 1949 - Neil was called into action with the Navy to go fight in the Korean War.
  • 1952 - Neil Armstrong joins NASA after returning from the war. Neil began working for what would later become NASA
  • 1956 - Neil gets married to his wife Janet Shearon
  • 1962 - Neil moves his family to Texas so he could join and work in the astronaut program
  • 1966 - Neil and his flight mate, David Scott, were launched into space to dock with the Gemini Agena vehcile
  • 1969 - Neil Armstrong and two other astronauts landed on the moon.
  • 1969 - Neil and his crew are awarded for their efforts
  • 1971 - Armstrong leaves NASA to become a professor in Ohio at the university of Cincinnati
  • 1986 - Neil Armstrong helped investigate the failed space misson, the Challenger
  • 2006 - Neil liked his privacy and stayed out of the public eye for years. He gave a TV interview on 60 Minutes
  • 2010 - Armstrong speaks out about the space cuts made during Barack Obama's presidential run
  • 2012 - Neil Armstrong dies at the age of 82, after complications from heart surgery. His contributions to the space program, and our understanding of outer space, can't be measure. He will always be remembered for his giant step for mankind
